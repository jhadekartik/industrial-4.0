Introduction to Industrial IoT

What is Industrial IoT?

Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) is a subset of IoT that focuses specifically on industrial applications. It involves connecting industrial assets, sensors, and systems to collect, exchange, and analyze data for improved operational efficiency, productivity, and reliability.

Unlike consumer IoT, Industrial IoT typically has more stringent requirements for reliability, security, and durability, as it often operates in harsh environments and supports critical infrastructure.

Key Characteristics of IIoT

  • Real-time Monitoring: Continuous tracking of industrial processes and equipment
  • Predictive Capabilities: Anticipating failures and maintenance needs
  • Autonomous Operation: Systems that can function with minimal human intervention
  • Interoperability: Communication between diverse systems and devices
  • Scalability: Ability to expand and adapt as needs change
  • Reliability: High uptime and dependability for critical operations

IIoT Business Models

Product as a Service (PaaS)

Selling the outcome or performance of a product rather than the product itself. Manufacturers retain ownership and responsibility for maintenance.

Example:

Instead of selling compressors, offering "compressed air as a service" with payment based on volume used.

Predictive Maintenance

Offering maintenance services based on real-time condition monitoring and predictive analytics, reducing downtime and extending equipment life.

Example:

Monitoring vibration patterns in motors to predict failures and schedule maintenance before breakdowns occur.

Data as a Service

Collecting, analyzing, and selling data insights from industrial processes to help customers optimize their operations.

Example:

Providing benchmarking data across multiple facilities to identify best practices and improvement opportunities.

Platform Business Model

Creating ecosystems where multiple stakeholders can connect, share data, and create value through applications and services.

Example:

Industrial cloud platforms that enable third-party developers to create applications for specific industry needs.
